We perspire to inspire.

We are working towards building a self-sustainable village, to start with we started building a self-sustained family. We as a family started natural farming in 2015, with all support from Gramabhartahi and Padma Sri Shubash Palekar Natural Farming model.

We grow and Conserve Heirlooms

  • Desi Rice Varities - 130+
  • Toor dal
  • Pumpkin
  • Greengram
  • Blackgram
  • Cowpea (White and Brown)
  • Hyacinth (Anumulu)
  • Sesame (White and Black)
  • Jowar (White and Yellow)
  • Bengal gram
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Finger Millet
  • Groundnut
  • Mustard
  • Chilli - 40+

In the given list of seeds above, rice varieties, collected from different sources all over India from 2016. in the year 2021, we have got 15 chilli varities from Brazil and 25 varities from others. We have more passion for conserving the "Sampradhya Beej" in the coming years.

We Sell What We Eat

We cultivate most of the above crops for our family consumption and a few crops for selling. We are not certified by any authority. We are self-certified natural farmers, work for soil health and purity of the seed.

Our Story

We have started natural farming in 2015, learned about ZBNF(SPNF now) through youtube, and attended the ZBNF session in Karimnagar conducted by Gramabharathi, Telangana. Disbelief to belief happened slowly year by year.

  • 2015

    Unlearning What We Have Learned


    (June - May) - 1/4 acre:

    Started with 1/4 acre of Vegetables, some other kitchen needs, and Onions for family consumption, we consume them till the next crop.

  • 2016

    GAF(Grandfather Approval Factor) : 3 out of 5


    (June - May) - 2.5 acres:

    • Applied to 3rd term Sugarcane (2.5 acres), got yield almost equal to chemical farming
    • Applied to groundnuts and above-mentioned kitchen needs, yield was satisfactory - (Grandfather Approval Factor - GAF)
    • Applied to 1 acre of Rice (1010) not satisfied - GAF

    GAF - 3
  • 2017

    Troublesome year in natural farming history in our family


    5 acres:

    • In the rainy season, we have grown 5 acres, all 5 desi seeds(Mysuru Malliga, Bhahurupi, Maappilai Samba, Kalabhatti, Kullakar). No one buys, no one is aware of these rice varieties at all. An article has come in "Raithu Nestham" few consumers took rice. One retired employee from our district took some amount of paddy.
    • We have given rice for free to our relatives to eat, as they don't know the importance they tried a few times and left as is, we finally used that for chicken feed. Now(2020) people are buying at Rs. 150/kg. Only one variety "Mysuru Malliga" has demand and yield is also good

    GAF - 0, no acceptance, because no sales

  • 2018

    Baby steps in getting Consumers


    6 acres:

    • We have grown only two varieties Mysuru Malliga, Dhudheshwar, and conserved 3 other varieties in smaller area
    • Sakshi Main Edition Article has come on our natural farming practice, Many calls from many people. We were able to sell all the rice.

    GAF - 2, Accepted by family, but not all 6 acres.
  • 2019

    Better Than Before


    6 acres :

    • People getting to know about Mysuru Malliga, Dhudheshwar varieties and their nutritional value, everything sold.

    GAF - 4, Satisfied

  • 2020

    Conserving the Heirloom Seeds


    9 acres:

    • We are cultivating three varieties for selling Rathnachaudi, Mysuru Malliga, Dhudheshwar
    • We turned into seeds conservators, we have collected 22 varieties of heirloom rice seeds. Sri Associates helped us to collect about 18 varieties of seeds.
    • Started noting the properties/characteristics of the Rice. International Rice Research Institute recommends 60-65 morphological properties of the rice with which we can identify the rice variety and remove the ambiguity. We started noting the properties.
    • We have got recognition as a representative for 80% of the farmers in India who can double their income in farming by SPNF method in NITI Aayog.
    • Article on Back To Nature about our Farm
